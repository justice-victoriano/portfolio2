THETA PHI ALPHA RECRUITMENT VIDEO

As the videographer of Theta Phi Alpha at The College of New Jersey, I was tasked with creating a Spring Recruitment video that would promote the sorority to potential new members. I collected clips from the members, filmed the introduction and ending, and edited the video. The video is posted on the organization’s official YouTube page and shared on Instagram and Facebook.

THE PROCESS

Collecting footage from the members of the organization was the most difficult part of the project since I was relying on them for the majority of the content. Since the clips came from different people, it was important that I added something that would make the video feel more cohesive. I decided to film an introduction and an ending with my Canon T7i. After I collected all the footage, I edited and color corrected the video in Premiere. I incorporated jump cuts, match cuts, strobe effects, transitions, and animations.

Title was done in After Effects.

The zoom effect was done by layering the polaroid video (video 1) and the video of the girl (video 2). I masked video 1 and gradually changed the opacity of video 2 as the camera was getting closer to the polaroid. I also added a blur and a flashing light effect to further dramatize the zoom effect.

I incorporated pre-made animations and masked them to appear behind people in the clips—I wanted the animations to seem as though they were completely immersed in the scene.
